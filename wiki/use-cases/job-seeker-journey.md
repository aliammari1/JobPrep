## 💡 Key Success Factors

### What Worked Well

1. **Consistent Practice**
   - Daily 1-2 hour practice sessions
   - Weekend deep dives (4-6 hours)
   - Spaced repetition of weak areas

2. **Data-Driven Improvement**
   - Reviewed analytics weekly
   - Focused on weak areas
   - Tracked progress metrics

3. **Realistic Simulation**
   - Used video interviews to simulate real conditions
   - Practiced with interview clothes
   - Created distraction-free environment

4. **AI Feedback Integration**
   - Acted on specific feedback immediately
   - Reviewed feedback before each real interview
   - Tracked improvement over time

5. **Strategic Applications**
   - Applied to mix of reach/target/safety companies
   - Tailored each application
   - Timed applications strategically

### Challenges Overcome

1. **Initial Nervousness**
   - Solution: 10+ practice interviews before first real interview
   - Used breathing techniques suggested by AI

2. **System Design Gaps**
   - Solution: Dedicated week for system design study
   - Created personal framework and templates

3. **Rejection Handling**
   - Solution: Treated rejections as learning opportunities
   - Reviewed what went wrong, practiced more

4. **Time Management**
   - Solution: 2-hour daily blocks
   - Used Pomodoro technique for focus

## 🎯 Alex's Recommendations

### For Other Job Seekers

**"Start Earlier Than You Think"**
> "I wish I had started 2-3 months before actively applying. The practice compounds over time."

**"Track Everything"**
> "Use the analytics. I tracked my weak points and saw real improvement week over week. That motivated me to keep going."

**"Quality Over Quantity"**
> "Don't just do 100 practice interviews. Do 30 great ones, review the recordings, and implement the feedback."

**"Customize Everything"**
> "The AI-generated cover letters were great starting points, but I personalized each one. That made a huge difference."

**"Embrace Failure"**
> "I failed the Meta onsite, but I learned so much. Applied those lessons to Airbnb and crushed it."

### Advice for Beginners

1. **Week 1**: Focus on CV and baseline skills
2. **Week 2-4**: Heavy practice on weak areas
3. **Week 5-6**: Start applying while practicing
4. **Week 7-9**: Interview sprint
5. **Throughout**: Review, iterate, improve
