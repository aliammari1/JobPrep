# Corporate Training Case Study

## 🏢 TechGlobal Internal Mobility Program

### Company Profile
- **Company**: TechGlobal Inc.
- **Industry**: Enterprise Software
- **Size**: 8,500 employees globally
- **Engineering**: 2,800 engineers (33% of workforce)
- **Locations**: 12 offices across US, Europe, Asia
- **Challenge**: Stagnant internal mobility, high external hiring costs

## 🎯 The Business Problem (Q4 2024)

### Internal Promotion Challenges

**The Situation**:
- Engineers stayed in same role 4+ years
- Internal promotion rate: Only 8% annually
- 70% of senior/staff roles filled externally
- High recruiting costs: $15k per external hire
- Knowledge loss from external hires
- Employee satisfaction declining

**Why Internal Promotions Weren't Happening**:
1. Engineers didn't know how to "interview" internally
2. Managers didn't have time to prepare candidates
3. No structured preparation for level-up interviews
4. Behavioral question skills weak (engineers focused on coding)
5. No practice opportunities without "real" stakes

**Financial Impact**:
- External senior hires: 200/year × $15k = $3M recruiting
- Turnover from blocked careers: 150/year × $150k cost = $22.5M
- Lost productivity during knowledge transfer
- **Total annual cost**: ~$30M

### Employee Sentiment

**Exit Interview Themes**:
> "I wanted to grow here, but there was no clear path."

> "I didn't get the promotion, so I interviewed elsewhere and got it."

> "The company invests more in hiring than developing internal talent."

**Engagement Survey (Q4 2024)**:
- Career growth satisfaction: 5.2/10
- Internal opportunity awareness: 4.8/10
- Likelihood to recommend company: 6.1/10

## 💡 Solution: JobPrep-Powered Mobility Program (Q1 2025)

### Program Design

**"Level Up" Internal Mobility Program**:
- Launched Q1 2025
- Powered by JobPrep AI Enterprise
- Available to all engineering employees
- Focus: IC4 → IC5/IC6, IC5 → Staff/Principal

### Three-Tier Approach

#### Tier 1: Self-Service Preparation
**Target**: All engineers interested in advancement
- JobPrep Enterprise access ($25/employee/year)
- Self-paced interview preparation
- On-demand practice
- Analytics dashboard

**Features Enabled**:
- Mock interviews (all levels)
- System design practice
- Leadership behavioral questions
- Code challenge arena
- Interview recording and review

#### Tier 2: Manager-Supported Track
**Target**: Engineers nominated by managers
- Everything in Tier 1
- Monthly 1:1 with manager reviewing JobPrep analytics
- Internal mentor assigned
- Quarterly promotion readiness assessment

**Manager Tools**:
- Dashboard to monitor direct report progress
- Interview rubrics aligned with company levels
- Feedback integration with JobPrep

#### Tier 3: Fast Track Program
**Target**: High-potential engineers
- Everything in Tier 1 & 2
- Dedicated career coach
- Mock panel interviews
- Executive sponsor
- 6-month intensive program

### Implementation Timeline

**Month 1 (January 2025)**:
- Pilot with 50 engineers
- Internal announcement and buzz-building
- Manager training sessions
- JobPrep customization (company values, leadership principles)

**Month 2 (February 2025)**:
- Expand to 500 engineers
- Company-wide launch event
- Department ambassadors identified
- Success metrics baseline established

**Month 3-6 (March-June 2025)**:
- Full rollout to all 2,800 engineers
- Quarterly promotion cycles aligned
- First cohort completes program
- Iterate based on feedback

## 📚 Lessons Learned

### What Worked Exceptionally Well

1. **Executive Sponsorship**
   - VP championed program
   - Resources allocated
   - Metrics tracked at leadership level

2. **Manager Engagement**
   - Dashboard gave managers actionable insights
   - 1:1s became more effective
   - Promotion conversations more objective

3. **Normalization of Practice**
   - "Everyone uses JobPrep" culture
   - No stigma in preparation
   - Practice = sign of ambition, not weakness

4. **Data-Driven Decisions**
   - Objective readiness scores
   - Reduced bias in promotion decisions
   - Clear feedback for unsuccessful candidates

### Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1**: Initial skepticism
- *"AI can't replace human judgment"*
- **Solution**: Positioned as prep tool, not replacement. Managers still decide, but candidates better prepared.

**Challenge 2**: Uneven adoption across teams
- Some managers didn't engage
- **Solution**: Leadership mandated manager participation, tied to manager performance reviews

**Challenge 3**: Promotion panel consistency
- Interviews still varied in difficulty
- **Solution**: Standardized rubrics, panel training, JobPrep insights shared with panels

**Challenge 4**: Fast Track creating tension
- "Why wasn't I selected?"
- **Solution**: Clear criteria, transparent selection, Tier 1 & 2 available to all

## 🎯 Playbook for Other Companies

### Getting Started (Months 1-3)

**Month 1: Foundation**
- [ ] Secure executive sponsor
- [ ] Define success metrics
- [ ] Purchase licenses
- [ ] Customize question banks
- [ ] Integrate with HR systems

**Month 2: Pilot**
- [ ] Select 50-100 pilot users
- [ ] Train managers
- [ ] Launch pilot
- [ ] Collect feedback weekly

**Month 3: Iteration**
- [ ] Analyze pilot data
- [ ] Refine program
- [ ] Plan full rollout
- [ ] Create internal marketing

### Scaling (Months 4-12)

**Rollout Strategy**:
1. Department by department (not all at once)
2. Nominate ambassadors in each team
3. Celebrate early wins loudly
4. Track metrics monthly
5. Iterate based on data

**Manager Enablement**:
- Quarterly manager workshops
- Best practice sharing
- Dashboard training
- Coaching on coaching

### Success Metrics to Track

**Leading Indicators**:
- Enrollment rate
- Active users (weekly)
- Mock interviews per user
- Manager engagement with dashboards

**Lagging Indicators**:
- Internal promotion rate
- External hire rate (senior roles)
- Promotion success rate
- Employee satisfaction (career growth)
- Retention (high performers)
- Time to promotion

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Making it optional**: Low adoption, limited impact
2. **Skipping manager training**: Managers don't engage
3. **No exec sponsor**: Lacks priority, resources
4. **Ignoring data**: Missing insights, iteration
5. **Overselling**: Setting unrealistic expectations
6. **Underselling**: Employees don't take seriously
